Data & charts

Homelessness by the numbers

National data from HUD's Annual Homeless Assessment Report (AHAR) and Point-in-Time counts. All charts are interactive — hover for details.

National Homeless Population, 2015–2024

HUD Point-in-Time Count — one-night snapshot, January each year. 2021 count was significantly disrupted by COVID-19.

Homelessness rose 18% from 2023 to 2024, reaching 771,480 — the highest count on record since HUD began tracking in 2007.

Sheltered vs. Unsheltered, 2015–2024

People in emergency shelters, transitional housing, and Safe Havens are "sheltered." Sleeping outside, in cars, or abandoned buildings is "unsheltered."

Sheltered capacity expanded sharply in 2024 — the unsheltered share fell from 39% in 2023 to 36% in 2024, even as the overall total hit a new record high.

Who Experiences Homelessness (2023)

HUD 2023 PIT Count — national totals by subpopulation. Numbers are not mutually exclusive (a veteran family counts in multiple categories).

Chronically homeless individuals — those with long-term or repeated homelessness and a disabling condition — represent 20% of the total but consume a disproportionate share of resources.

Race: US Population vs. Homeless Population

Compares each group's share of the general US population to their share of the homeless population. Source: HUD 2023 AHAR, US Census.

The most striking disparity: Black Americans are 13% of the US population but 37% of people experiencing homelessness — a 3× overrepresentation rooted in decades of discriminatory housing policy.
Reports & research

Primary sources and key reports

The most important data sources on homelessness — linked directly so you can go to the source.

HUD Annual Homeless Assessment Report (AHAR) The definitive federal report on homelessness in America — published annually with Point-in-Time count data and detailed subpopulation breakdowns. Read the report → USICH All In: The Federal Strategic Plan to Prevent and End Homelessness The Biden-era federal strategy for ending homelessness — sets goals, outlines evidence-based interventions, and benchmarks progress. Read the plan → NAEH State of Homelessness Report National Alliance to End Homelessness tracks trends across all 50 states — includes interactive state comparisons and annual trend data. Explore the data → HUD Exchange Point-in-Time Count Data Raw CoC-level PIT count data for every Continuum of Care in the US — downloadable by year going back to 2007. Download data → NLIHC The Gap: A Shortage of Affordable Homes National Low Income Housing Coalition's annual analysis of the affordable housing shortage — the structural driver behind rising homelessness. Read the report → HUD Exchange CoC Map & Directory Interactive map of all ~400 Continuums of Care in the US — click any CoC to see funded programs, contact information, and performance data. Explore the map → California DHCS CARE Court: Annual Report 2025 California's compelled treatment program through its first year — 557 petitions filed, 100 participants with plans or agreements, $713K estimated cost per participant. Essential reading for comparing compelled vs. voluntary approaches. Read the report →
