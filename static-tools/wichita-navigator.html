🎖️ Veteran Homeless Services
Wichita / Sedgwick County, KS
Verified
Robert J. Dole VA Medical Center — Homeless Programs
5500 E. Kellogg Dr., Wichita, KS 67218
Main: (316) 685-2221 — ask for the Homeless Veteran Care Coordinator or Health Care for Homeless Veterans (HCHV)
Services: outreach, HUD-VASH vouchers, case management, VA healthcare enrollment, and transitional housing referrals for veterans who are homeless or at risk. Social work staff available Mon–Fri.
United Way of the Plains 211 — Coordinated Entry
Call 211 or text your ZIP code to 898-211
Veterans are eligible for both VA housing programs and the local Continuum of Care (KS-502). 211 connects you to the Coalition to End Homelessness coordinated-entry assessment so you can be screened for both at once. Free, confidential, 24/7.
Veterans Crisis Line
Call 988 and press 1 — free, confidential, 24/7.
National Call Center for Homeless Veterans: 1-877-424-3838
Confidence: Verified — sourced from va.gov/wichita-health-care, wichita.gov, and unitedwayplains.org. Phone numbers verified from .gov/.org sources. Last checked: June 2026.
🌱 Youth Homeless Services (24 & under)
Wichita / Sedgwick County, KS
Verified
Wichita Children's Home — CrossRoads Shelter
7271 E. 37th St. N., Wichita, KS 67226
(316) 684-6581
The community's only 24-hour low-barrier emergency shelter for youth ages 18–24 experiencing homelessness. Immediate help: food, hygiene supplies, showers, clothing, laundry, mail service, health care, WiFi and computers. The 30+ day program builds a plan toward stable housing, education, and employment.
Wichita Children's Home — Youth Crisis Hotline & Safe Place
If you are 24 or under and need help now: call (316) 262-HOME (316-262-4663).
Safe Place: text the word SAFE and your location to 44357, or walk into any area QuikTrip (a designated Safe Place site) and ask staff to call for you.
United Way of the Plains 211 — After-Hours Backstop
Call or text 211 (or text your ZIP to 898-211) — 24/7. For shelter referrals and youth services across Sedgwick County when other access points are closed.
Confidence: Verified — sourced from wch.org and unitedwayplains.org. CrossRoads is the sole dedicated 18–24 youth shelter in KS-502 as of June 2026. Last checked: June 2026.

🏠 Permanent Supportive Housing
Chronically Homeless — Wichita / Sedgwick County, KS
Research
United Way of the Plains 211 — Coordinated Entry (Required First Step)
Permanent supportive housing in KS-502 is accessed through the Coalition to End Homelessness coordinated-entry system. You must be assessed to be placed on the housing referral list.

Start here: call 211 or text your ZIP to 898-211. A 211 specialist will ask about your situation and route you to a housing navigator. Providers meet weekly to ensure every assessed household has a housing plan.
City of Wichita — Housing First Program
(316) 462-3724 (Shelly Haupt, Housing & Community Services)
Provides rent and utility assistance plus case management to place chronically homeless individuals into permanent rental housing. Uses HUD's chronic-homelessness definition (a disabling condition and homeless for a year or in four-plus episodes over three years). Participants meet weekly with a case manager and contribute up to 30% of income toward rent.
HumanKind Ministries — The Inn (Shelter While You Wait)
320 E. Central Ave., Wichita, KS 67202 · (316) 264-8051
24-hour emergency shelter with case management to keep you safe and connected while you await a housing placement. Beds first come, first served — call ahead.
988 — Mental Health & Crisis Support
Call or text 988 — free, 24/7. If a behavioral health condition is part of your situation, 988 can connect you to Sedgwick County COMCARE crisis services, which also support disability-benefit (SOAR) and treatment access tied to supportive housing.
Confidence: Research — sourced from wichita.gov/homelessness-programs and unitedwayplains.org. PSH placements are waitlisted; coordinated-entry assessment is required before any referral. Last checked: June 2026.
