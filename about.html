Every rung, together.

Common Ladder is a civic resource platform. We believe housing stability is a community achievement — not an individual one.

Our mission

The ladder the whole community builds.

There are hundreds of shelters, programs, nonprofits, and government services working on homelessness in any given city. But there's no single place where anyone can find all of them, understand how they connect, and navigate to the right one.

Common Ladder changes that. Whether you need a bed tonight, you're a case manager coordinating services for fifty clients, or you're a city official trying to understand what's working — Common Ladder is the platform that makes the whole system visible and navigable.

We don't run shelters or provide direct services. We build infrastructure: the map, the directory, and the navigation layer that makes everything else work better.

What we believe

How we approach the work

🪜

Everyone belongs on the ladder

People experiencing homelessness are members of the community — not separate from it. The platform is designed with dignity, not charity, as the organizing principle.

📱

Mobile first, always

The majority of people accessing help will be on a phone. Every tool we build starts at 375px and works without reliable internet or a charged battery.

🔓

No barriers to access

No login required. No forms to fill out before you can search. No account needed to find a shelter. Information is a public good.

📊

Data-driven, community-built

We work in partnership with local nonprofits, Continuums of Care, and people with lived experience to keep our information accurate, current, and trustworthy.
